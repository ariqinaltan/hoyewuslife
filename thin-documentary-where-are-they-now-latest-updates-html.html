Thin Documentary Where Are They Now? Latest Updates

Spread the love             

Hey there, wonderful readers! Today, let’s dive into the powerful world of the documentary “Thin.” Directed by Lauren Greenfield and sprinkled with HBO magic, this film takes us on an emotional journey through the struggles of four incredible women battling eating disorders at The Renfrew Center of Florida. Join me as we catch up on where these brave souls are now!

The Heartfelt Stories of Courage

Picture this: a documentary that captures the raw, unfiltered reality of four women facing anorexia nervosa, bulimia nervosa, and other eating disorders. “Thin” landed in our hearts in 2006, revealing the highs and lows of recovery at The Renfrew Center.

Alisa Williams – The Journey of Resilience

Alisa, a 30-year-old mother of two, faced severe challenges, from divorce to battling binge/purge sessions. Dealing with hospitalizations due to excessive use of diuretics, laxatives, and more, Alisa’s journey with Renfrew initially showed promise. Despite expressing a desire to recover, post-discharge, she struggled. The epilogue shares her setbacks, including a suicide attempt, yet ends on a hopeful note with her return to Renfrew, ultimately achieving a healthier weight.

 Brittany Robinson – Navigating Teen Turbulence

Brittany, a 15-year-old student, entered Renfrew with liver damage, hair loss, and a low heart rate. Her battle with anorexia stemmed from a history of compulsive overeating, driven by a “bad body image” and a desire for peer acceptance. Brittany’s story unfolds with her insurance benefits running out, forcing her to leave treatment early. Unfortunately, the epilogue reveals her relapse into anorexia post-discharge.

 Shelly Guillory – The Nurse in Need

Meet Shelly, a psychiatric nurse, grappling with anorexia for six years, relying on tube feeding and facing mood swings. Post-discharge, she lost weight, underwent electroconvulsive therapy, and battled accusations. Shelly’s journey reflects the complexities of her condition, challenging the stereotypes associated with eating disorders.

 Polly Ann Williams – A Struggle with Demons

Polly Ann, known as Pollack, checked into Renfrew after a suicide attempt over two slices of pizza. Struggling with anorexia since age 11, she faced expulsion after getting a tattoo on a day pass and providing medications to Shelly. Polly’s post-Renfrew struggles continued, marked by purging and weight loss, leading to her unfortunate passing in February 2008.

Brittany’s Relapse and Unfortunate Hurdles

Brittany, with a resistance to recovery, aimed to lose “another forty pounds” and left treatment early due to insurance limitations. Post-discharge, she rapidly lost weight, facing a relapse into anorexia. However, insurance hurdles obstructed her path to further treatment.

Wrapping Up: Real Lives, Real Struggles

And there you have it – a heartfelt update on the incredible women we met in the “Thin” documentary. Their stories are a testament to the resilience of the human spirit in the face of immense challenges. Let’s keep these warriors in our thoughts and celebrate the courage they’ve shown on their unique journeys. Until next time, stay strong and spread kindness!
