Mary Barra Biography and Net worth, Salary and Husband Anthony Barra

Mary Barra is the beautiful and smart CEO of the famous Fortune 500 company General Motors in the United States. Since 2014, she has become the first woman to lead a motor vehicle manufacturing company anywhere in the world.

Barra’s history with General Motors began at a pretty incredible age before she even went to college perhaps; this contributed to her success story and also earned her the qualifications and experience to run such a business even ahead of her male counterparts.

Biography of Mary Barra

Barra was born to Ray and Eva Makela in Royal Oak, Michigan on Christmas Eve 1961. Her parents are of Finnish descent. His ancestors are said to be from Kankaanpää in the Satakunta Province of Köyliö. She is not unique and has a brother, Paul Makela. Interestingly, his father worked for the Pontiac auto plant in Detroit for four decades. This is how Marie developed a love of automobiles.

She attended schools in Waterford, Waterford, Michigan. After graduating from Waterford Mott High School, she found a job in an automotive industry in order to pay her tuition. Mary Barra would later earn a BSc in Electrical Engineering from Kettering University (formerly the General Motors Institute). She also earned an MBA from the Stanford Graduate School of Business in 1990.

In 2014, she received an honorary degree from the University of Michigan after delivering the spring commencement address for its Ann Arbor campus at Michigan Stadium.

Barra began her career in 1980 with GeneralMotors as a work-study student, her duties later included managing the Detroit/Hamtramck assembly plant. Through hard work and determination, she began to rise. In 2008, she was elected Vice President of Global Manufacturing Engineering and then Global Human Resources the following year.

Mary was elected Chair of the Board of General Motors Corporation on January 4, 2014. She currently serves as the Chief Executive Officer of General Motors since January 15, 2016.

She is a globe taker who is eager to see the change the world deserves. Within her offices, Mary Barra entrusted her team with responsibility for the design, engineering and launch of General Motors worldwide. She is an astute and inflexible leader who, through her leadership, brought good news to General Motors during her takeover as CEO of General Motors.

General Motors even easily beat its competitor, Ford, with a profit of 3.6% before taxes and interest. In 2017, their average vehicle sale price reached a record high of $36,000

This strong General Motors earnings could only be possible with the unwavering support and leadership of Mary Barra. She currently serves on the board of directors of the Walt Disney Company, the board of trustees of Stanford University and the Detroit Economic Club.

She is also a member of the Business Council and a member of the At-Large Board of Directors at the Business Roundtable where she also sits on the Social Issues Committee. In 2014, Times magazine named her one of the “100 most influential people in the world”. Two years later (2016), she was appointed to a business forum committee convened by Donald Trump to provide policy and strategic advice on economic issues. She quit the following year, however, following Trump’s reaction to the Charlottesville protests.

In 2019, Mary Barra emerged for the first time in the automotive and auto parts industry in Institutional Investor ‘s annual survey of senior executives. Isn’t Mary Barra just your ordinary wife?

Salary and net worth

In February 2019, it was revealed that the beautiful and smart blonde CEO’s net worth had reached $64 million.

It’s no surprise though as the smart lady in the automotive industry has made quite an impression with her hard work and determination. She currently owns over 39,095 General Motors units worth an impressive $42,063,158, in addition to the $21,958,000 she earns as Chairman and CEO of General Motors. .

Who is Mary Barra’s husband? – Anthony Barra

Mary Barra is married to Anthony Barra, an engineering consultant she met while studying at Kettering University. They are both involved in humanitarian work. In 2014, they both chaired the Detroit International Wine Auction. Through the College for Creative Studies, they have also brought together community arts programs and scholarships worth $2.4 million.

They are proud parents of two children, Nicholas and Rachel Barra. The couple lives in Northville (suburb of Detroit).
