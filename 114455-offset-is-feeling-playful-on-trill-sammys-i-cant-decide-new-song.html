Offset Is Feeling Playful On Trill Sammy's &quot;I Can't Decide&quot;

Trill Sammy represents a less sequestered portion of Houston's rap subculture. The young rapper has shown a willingness to venture far and away to attract potential suitors. On his debut EP No Sleep Vol. 1, Sammy is able to corral a bunch of high profile feats from the likes of NBA Youngboy, J.I.D, among others.

On "I Can't Decide" Sammy links with one third of the travelling Migos troupe. Offset's 2nd verse is essentially over before it begins, such is the case with rap complacency in 2018, especially when it concerns Offset and "lead vocalist" Quavo Huncho. That being said, Trill Sammy makes the most of this outing to separate himself from this notion of complacency by putting on a clinic of sorts. "I Can't Decide" is produced by a consortium of unlikely collaborators that includes Jake One, Southside, Sam Wish and virtual "party guy" Benny Blanco. Check out "I Can't Decide" and hit us with your thoughts.
